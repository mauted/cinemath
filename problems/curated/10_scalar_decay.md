# Scalar decay (QFT warm-up)

Consider the Lagrangian involving two real scalar fields $\Phi$ and $\phi$:

$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2} (\partial_\mu \Phi)^2 - \frac{1}{2} M^2 \Phi^2 + \frac{1}{2} (\partial_\mu \phi)^2 - \frac{1}{2} m^2 \phi^2 - \mu \Phi \phi \phi$

The last term allows a $\Phi$ particle to decay into two $\phi$'s when $M > 2m$.
Assuming that condition, compute to lowest order in $\mu$ the decay rate $\Gamma$ of $\Phi$,
and find the lifetime $\tau = 1/\Gamma$.
